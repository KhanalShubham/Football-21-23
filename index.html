Methodology

Sourced via FBref & Opta (2020-2023). Data verified via FBref.com.

Our Era Impact Score normalizes output across leagues using UEFA coefficients and market value data. Striker goals are weighted differently from midfielder chances created. Discipline and defensive work rate are factored into the final calculation.

Key Metrics

  • Key Pass: A pass that directly creates a shot or a clear scoring chance per 90 minutes.
  • Recovery: Successful regains of possession (tackles, interceptions, loose balls) per 90 minutes.
  • Discipline: Negative events such as fouls, bookings, and errors leading to shots or goals.

Why De Bruyne?

Kevin De Bruyne ranks #1 because this model places a high weight on creative volume (Key Passes + Assists) rather than pure goal scoring. His elite chance creation stacked across multiple seasons generates more cumulative Impact Points than even the best pure finishers.

In other words, repeated high-value creation for teammates is treated as just as valuable as scoring the goals yourself in this specific weighted framework.

Model Perspective

The Era Impact Engine v3 leans into repeatable process over single-season peaks. Players who consistently drive shot volume, territory, and possession over multiple years accumulate more points than one‑season outliers.

Creative hubs like De Bruyne benefit because every key pass and assisted shot is rewarded. Pure scorers only close the gap if their goals arrive at extreme volume while still contributing in link play and defensive work.

  • Mohamed Salah (Liverpool) – Monster scorer and chance creator; narrowly misses the very top tier once defensive and league‑adjusted weights are applied.
